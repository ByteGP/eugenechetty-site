The brief is always the same. Senior marketing hire. Proven track record. Strong brand experience, ideally FMCG. Exceptional stakeholder management.

And every time, the hire fails within eighteen months.

Not because the person is bad at marketing. Because they're great at the wrong kind of marketing.

## The category error

Energy is not a consumer goods category. It is not a brand love category. It is not a category where a great campaign changes buying behaviour in any meaningful way.

It is a category where most customers join reluctantly, stay by default, and leave only when something goes wrong. The purchase journey looks nothing like a funnel. The competitive advantage sits in operations, pricing, and service — not in creative execution.

But the marketing briefs keep asking for FMCG marketers. People who understand brand equity, above-the-line investment, and consumer psychology in categories where those things matter.

## What the category actually needs

The translator problem in energy is specific: you need someone who can walk into a room with the pricing team, understand the commercial model in enough detail to have a real conversation, then walk into a creative briefing and communicate why the constraints exist — and what's still possible within them.

Most marketing hires can do one or the other. The FMCG candidate has the creative fluency but no instinct for the commercial architecture. The digital performance hire understands the numbers but can't build a brand story that holds attention for more than a click.

The hire that works is the one who's genuinely comfortable in both rooms. Not a generalist — a translator.

## What this means for the brief

If you're hiring a senior marketing leader for a regulated, commoditised category, the questions worth asking look different:

Can they explain why a campaign isn't working in financial terms, not just marketing terms? Can they push back on a pricing team with creative arguments? Do they have an instinct for which brand investments compound over time and which ones are just spend?

Those questions filter for translators. The standard brief doesn't even come close.
